The Oystercatcher Slackpack Guided Hiking Trail

The Oystercatcher Trail is a world-renowned hiking experience, offering a unique combination of nature conservation and adventure along the pristine South Cape coastline. Spanning from the Gouritz River Mouth to Mossel Bay, this guided slackpacking trail allows hikers to immerse themselves in breathtaking landscapes without the burden of heavy backpacks.

Founded in 2002, the Oystercatcher Trail was one of the first to introduce the concept of slackpacking. The trail, designed for practicality and environmental conservation, was established to protect the endangered Black Oystercatcher and the coastal ecosystem. Over the years, it has earned international recognition, being listed as one of the top 50 hikes globally by outlets like the BBC and Getaway magazine.

As you hike this 28-46 km stretch of untouched coastline, you'll encounter the Big 5 of the ocean, including whales that grace the waters during cooler months. The trail also offers an abundance of bird species and a vibrant display of Fynbos, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts.

With only a small day-pack to carry, this slackpacking adventure lets you focus on the stunning surroundings. Whether you're traversing the dunes or walking the beach, this experience will change your perspective on nature and leave you with lasting memories.
