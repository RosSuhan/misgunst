Bird Watching at Voëlvlei Pan - A Hidden Gem

Voëlvlei Pan, located near Misgunst Farm, is a must-visit destination for bird lovers. This temporary wetland, although dry for most years, transforms into an incredible birding hotspot during wet periods, attracting a variety of migratory waders. The pan becomes especially lively in the summer, making it one of the top birding locations in the Garden Route.

Birdwatching here is best done by vehicle, as the birds are less threatened by cars compared to humans on foot. A dirt road connects the two entry points along the northern shoreline, offering excellent viewing opportunities. Scan the pan every few hundred meters, and you might catch sight of the Black Harrier, Cape Clapper Lark, and the striking Orange-breasted Sunbird, among many other species.

Due to its location on private property, access to Voëlvlei Pan must be arranged through the Fransmanshoek Conservancy. This ensures the area remains protected, and birding enthusiasts can enjoy the landscape respectfully.

The Gouritz River, with its single-lane bridge, is another fantastic addition to the birding trip, offering unique perspectives of the local birdlife. From the Victorin's Warbler to the Knysna Woodpecker, Voëlvlei Pan provides a rare opportunity to observe some of South Africa's most sought-after bird species.
