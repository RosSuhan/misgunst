About Us

Nestled along the stunning southern coastline of the Western Cape, Misgunst Farm has been a family treasure for generations. The farm's rich history dates back to the 1700s when the French warship Le Fortune met its fate near Fransmanshoek. Pieces of this maritime history remain intertwined with the land, from cannons salvaged over the years to a bow beam from the ship itself embedded in the farm's original homestead fireplace.

But beyond its historical roots, Misgunst is a place where nature takes centre stage. Spanning vast dune systems, indigenous landscapes, and the breathtaking coastline of the Fransmanshoek Conservancy, the farm is a haven for those who seek adventure and tranquillity alike. As one of the largest dune systems in the Southern Cape, the farm offers visitors the eco-friendly Vleesbaai 4x4 Dune Route, where nature lovers can explore the scenic beauty of the dunes in a sustainable way.

With deep ties to the land and a commitment to conservation, the Janse van Rensburg family, owners of Misgunst, have worked to preserve the natural landscape while offering a peaceful retreat for visitors. From the preservation of local ecosystems to the maintenance of the 14km stretch of coastline under the Fransmanshoek Conservancy, Misgunst is dedicated to safeguarding this unique environment for future generations.

Whether you're drawn by the farm's fascinating history or its unspoiled beauty, we invite you to experience it for yourself. Discover the perfect balance of adventure and relaxation at Misgunst, where nature, heritage, and hospitality combine to create an unforgettable holiday experience.
